Forecasting Variable

  • Renewable Energy Consumption

We choose renewable energy consumption as our forecasting variable. We are interested to see and try to predict renewable energy consumption in the United States in the years to come. One of the main reasons for that is that, the climate change today remains one of the most crucial issues for lawmakers and government agencies and scientist all over the world. Keeping in mind that there is a positive correlation between lung diseases in general and air pollution and renewable energy is a safe alternative for the climate issues and air pollution we thought it as an important variable to forecast. The measuring units are in quadrillion BTU (British thermal units), and the data is monthly from 1990 to 2015.

List of indepdent Variables

  • Industrial Production

Industrial production index was our other independent variable. The industrial production index data came for FRED, and as an index there is no measuring units. Our reason for choosing the INDPRO was to see if with the industrial development that USA has had in the last decades, we are moving more towards consuming renewable energy, keeping in mind that more industrial production means we need more resources of production and at the same time it means more technological advancement.

  • Temperature Change in United States

One of the independent variables we wanted to use was average monthly temperature in USA. The units are Celsius. As we all know according most scientists there is valuable data that there is a big increase in temperature during the last decade because of the climate change. We wanted to see if this negative phenomenon has had at least a positive impact such as that we are consuming more renewable energy trying to preserve the planet.

  • Average Retail Price of Electricity

Another independent variable we choose is the average retail price of electricity, we wanted to see if the prices of energy affect renewable energy consumption in any order, if when we have high prices, we turn to renewable energy as an alternative, and more renewable energy means more low prices. The units for this variable are cents/kilowatt-hour or as commonly written cents/kWh.

  • Co2 Emission

We wanted to see carbon dioxide emissions as an independent variable to see that effect that the volume of CO2 emissions has in the renewable energy consumption. We wanted to see a trend where less CO2 means we are consuming more renewable energy which logically should hold true. The data units are volume units and they are million metric tons of carbon dioxide. ## Time Series Plot of the Renewable Energy Consumption

We can see from the time series plot of renewable energy consumption that our data , has an a seasonal , and we also can see that the data is not stationary since it doesnt center around a constant mean. ## White Noise Test

We can see that with the results that we get from the Durbin-Watson test, our P-value is very small which means that we reject the null hypothesis in favor of alternative hypothesis , so there is serial autocorrelation in our model. Another way we can see that is by looking at the AFC for the residuals where can see that the AFC plot does not really die off, at the same time our residuals are not normally distributed and if we look at their plot we can even see almost a trend. VIF test tells us about the multicolinearity, since all of our VIF levels for the variables are less then 10 , then we can say that there is not a lot of multicolinearity that influences our model. However another failure of our regression model is that when look at the test for the white noise of the residuals, our residuals are not white noise , so that means that we cannot trust our model and that we will need to adjust it.
